how to find gems

Probably the most important attribute in finding gems is figuring out what you like and dislike in food through trial and error. Being curious about different kinds of food/cuisines also helps a lot. Finding places that mimic the flavors you had during your childhood is a good starting point. Also, be critical of certain sources offering advice on what "good food" is. Personal taste is well... personal, and if you enjoy the food you're eating, that's amazing!
As for finding places, I usually use these:

  • Google Maps & Google Reviews
  • TikTok
  • Instagram
  • r/foodies_sydney on Reddit
  • Word of mouth
  • Intuition

If a food content creator is deliberately trying to gatekeep a place, use Reverse Image Search to find it. As far as social media goes, use it as a starting point. If it makes you go "holy shit that looks good", do further digging by reading reviews online and potentially ask some friends how they feel about the place. Don't be afraid to ask strangers for recommendations if you're genuinely interested. Lastly, don't be afraid of slightly dodgy-looking food places. Sometimes your gut will tell you that the food is going to be amazing.
